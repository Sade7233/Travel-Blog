Why Are We All Here?

The desire to travel has always lived within my spirit, but I didn't end up taking my first official vacation until my senior year of college.

Through the years, my vacationless frustrations persisted as my list of travel destinations kept growing, but plans to vacation with my friends always fell through for one reason or another. Folks didn’t have their deposit money ready, they couldn't get the time off from work, et cetera, et cetera. I'm sure you all can relate! I eventually realized that if I kept waiting around for others to help my wanderlust dreams come true, it would never happen.

And so Google and Trip Advisor became my best friends. I researched, plotted like a maniac, and dove headfirst into solo travel early last year, starting with my trip to Chicago, IL:

What's So Great About (Solo) Travel, Anyway?

Travel can be whatever you want it to be. You can be taking a fight halfway across the world or taking a stroll through your own city - both are valid and both can be fulfilling. The most important aspect of travel is that you’re exploring your environment, learning about that space, and having fun along the way.

Solo travel has the added perks of independence and freedom to do and see whatever you want, whenever you want. It can be a little anxiety-inducing to think about at first, but it offers you the space and experiences for dramatic personal growth. Just think - if you can travel to another country solo, you can truly do ANYTHING!

What If I'm Not Ready to be Solo Dolo Yet?

That's totally okay! It took me some time to build up the courage to hit for my first solo vacation. And all of that was after much practice being solo-dolo in my own city.

It is my earnest hope that putting a spotlight on my (mis)adventures will bring a twinkle to your eye and soothe your own anxieties enough to help you step out of your comfort zone. If I can do it, so can you!

Operation: 30 by 30

My current travel goal is to take 30 unique vacations by my 30th birthday!

33.33%

Parameters for this goal are...

  1. Trips beginning with my first official vacation in 2016 and beyond are eligible
  2. A destination can only be counted once (I've been to Philly 3 times, but it only counts as 1/30 of my goal)
  3. Staycations/excursions in a hometown are eigible if I plan my trip as a tourist would
  4. Trip must last at least 12 hours in duration
  5. Trip must include at least 3 unique activities/excursions

Check back here for updates on my progress!
